Key Insights

Critical findings, concentration risks, and opportunities identified in the FY 2024 data

📊 Spending Concentration
The top 5 states (TX, CA, FL, NC, GA) account for 35.8% of all VA expenditures. The top 10 account for over half (51.1%), reflecting Veteran population distribution patterns.
⚠ Medical Care Dominance Risk
Medical Care represents 88.4% of indirect expenditures ($124.7B). This concentration creates budget vulnerability — any cost escalation in healthcare could significantly impact overall VA spending.
✅ Education Investment Opportunity
Education & Vocational Rehabilitation received $13.1B (9.3% of indirect), with outsized per-capita investment in states like Hawaii, Colorado, and Virginia — models for expanding GI Bill utilization nationally.
🚨 Compensation Growth Pressure
Compensation & Pension at $144.3B (93.4% of direct) reflects both expanded PACT Act eligibility and rising disability claims. This is the single largest spending category at 48.8% of all VA expenditures.
🏛 Infrastructure & Construction Disparity
Construction spending of $1.39B (1.0% of indirect) is heavily concentrated: Kentucky alone received $350M (25% of total construction), while 10 states received under $2M each. This suggests uneven infrastructure modernization that may impact service delivery equity.

Risks & Opportunities

Strategic observations for policy consideration

⚠ Geographic Access Gaps
Rural states like Wyoming ($847M), Vermont ($517M), and North Dakota ($732M) receive the lowest total expenditures. While proportional to Veteran populations, rural Veterans often face longer travel distances to VA facilities, potentially limiting healthcare access.
✅ High-Impact Education States
California ($1.8B), Texas ($1.6B), and Florida ($1.1B) lead in education spending, likely driven by large military-to-civilian transition pipelines near major installations. Targeted outreach in mid-tier states could further boost GI Bill utilization.
📊 States with Indirect > Direct Spending
28 states receive more in indirect benefits (medical, education) than direct payments. New York (1.28x), Ohio (1.22x), and Illinois (1.21x) lead — suggesting higher medical care utilization relative to compensation claims in these states.
⚠ Loan Guaranty Minimal Footprint
Loan Guaranty expenditures total only $42.4M (0.03% of indirect) — the smallest category. As housing affordability challenges grow nationwide, this program's limited spending may indicate underutilization of VA Home Loan benefits.

Data Notes & Methodology

State-level expenditures are rounded to the nearest thousand dollars. For programs associated with individuals, expenditures are based on the Veteran's state of residence. For non-individual programs (e.g., Construction), expenditures are reported by place of performance. FY 2024 is the first year that Memorial Benefits expenditures are included in GDX. This data may not be directly comparable with other VA financial reports due to differing criteria. DIC and Survivors Pension was also newly added as a separate category.
